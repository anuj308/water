Surface water Overview

Surface water refers to water sources visible on the Earth's surface, including rivers, lakes, reservoirs, ponds, and wetlands. These bodies of water play a crucial role in supporting various ecosystems, providing habitats for aquatic plants and animals, and serving as sources of drinking water, irrigation, recreation, and transportation for human communities. Surface water is replenished primarily by precipitation, which fills rivers and lakes through runoff and infiltration. However, surface water availability can vary seasonally and regionally, influenced by factors such as climate, topography, land use, and human activities such as dam construction and water diversion. Sustainable management of surface water resources is essential for maintaining ecological balance, supporting biodiversity, and meeting the needs of both human populations and the environment.
